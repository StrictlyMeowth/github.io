The AI Landscape

Who makes what, what it does, and how it all connects

The simplest mental model: Think of AI in three layers. Labs build the raw intelligence (models). Apps wrap that intelligence in a usable interface. You interact with apps, not models. The same lab can own multiple models and multiple apps — and apps often run on models from other labs.

How to stay on top of it all

The problem: AI is moving faster than any previous technology wave. New models drop every few weeks, apps rebrand, and labs merge or pivot. You don't need to know everything — you need a lightweight system that keeps you oriented without being overwhelming.
01
Use one mental map, not a list
Always ask: Is this a lab, a model, or an app? That one question cuts through 90% of the confusion. Labs make models. Apps deploy them. You use apps.
02
Follow one newsletter
The Rundown AI (therundown.ai) — daily, 5 minutes, covers everything that matters. For deeper dives: Ben's Bites or Import AI by Jack Clark.
03
Bookmark one benchmark site
lmarena.ai (formerly LMSYS Chatbot Arena) — real human head-to-head rankings. More reliable than any lab's self-reported benchmarks.
04
Know your personal stack
Pick 2–3 tools max. One for general chat. One for your work context (coding, writing, research). One for search. Don't chase every new release.
05
Check pricing quarterly
Prices drop fast as compute gets cheaper. Worth checking every 3 months — free tiers expand, new plans emerge. OpenRouter is good for API cost comparison.
06
Ignore most hype
Every new model is announced as the "GPT killer." Most aren't. Wait 2 weeks after any launch for independent benchmarks and real user feedback before changing your stack.
